Cyprus Visas 2026: Complete Guide to Entry into the Republic of Cyprus

Cyprus remains one of the most popular destinations for tourists, entrepreneurs, investors and families considering relocation to Europe. Everything about visas — based on official Cyprus Government data.

  • June 24, 2026
  • 10 min read
  • Artur Mardanyan
  • Official source: gov.cy

Do I Need a Visa to Enter Cyprus?

Whether you need a visa depends on your nationality. Citizens of the European Union (EU) and the European Economic Area (EEA) can enter Cyprus without a visa using either a passport or a national identity card.

Nationals of third countries may require a visa. The list of countries whose citizens require a visa is determined by EU legislation and the national legislation of the Republic of Cyprus.

Important: Cyprus is a European Union member state but is not yet a full member of the Schengen Area. A Cyprus visa is valid only within the Republic of Cyprus and does not grant freedom of movement across other Schengen countries.

Types of Cyprus Visas

The Republic of Cyprus issues several visa categories depending on the purpose and duration of the visit:

  • Airport Transit Visa (Category A) — for transit through the international zone of a Cyprus airport without passing through border control.
  • Short-Stay Visa (Category C) — for stays in Cyprus of up to 90 days.
  • Multiple Entry Visa (Category C MEV) — allows multiple entries during the validity period of the visa.
  • Border Visa — issued only in exceptional cases on humanitarian grounds.
  • Visa Extension — possibility to extend a valid visa where legal grounds exist.
  • Long-Stay Visa (Category D) — for stays in Cyprus exceeding 90 days for the purpose of work, study, business or family reunification. Depending on the purpose of stay, it may serve as the basis for obtaining a residence permit.

Official Cyprus visa category names: Airport Transit Visa (Category A), Short-Stay Visa (Category C), Multiple Entry Visa (Category C MEV), Long-Stay Visa (Category D).

Airport Transit Visa (Category A)

An airport transit visa is required for transit through the international zone of a Cyprus airport — without passing through border control or entering Cyprus territory.

Whether an Airport Transit Visa is required depends on the applicant's nationality and is determined by the current regulations of the Republic of Cyprus. Applicants in the following categories are exempt from obtaining an Airport Transit Visa:

  • Holders of valid Schengen visas or residence permits issued by certain countries are exempt from the Airport Transit Visa requirement.
  • The list of exemptions is updated regularly; it is therefore recommended to check the current requirements on the official website of the Cyprus Government before travelling.
Important

For your specific situation, please refer to the latest requirements published on the official website of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus, as the list of countries and the exemption conditions for Airport Transit Visas may change.

Official source: www.gov.cy/en/information/visas/

Short-Stay Visa (Category C)

The Short-Stay Visa (Category C) is intended for short-term stays in the Republic of Cyprus. This visa is most commonly used for tourism, business and personal visits.

Important: The Short-Stay Visa (Category C) permits a stay in the Republic of Cyprus of no more than 90 days within any 180-day period.

Long-Stay Visa (Category D) — First Step to a Residence Permit

If you plan to stay in Cyprus for more than 90 days for work, study, or other long-term purposes, you may need a Category D long-stay visa, depending on the purpose of your stay.

A Category D long-stay visa allows you to enter Cyprus for the purpose of subsequently obtaining the appropriate residence permit, if required for your purpose of stay.

After entering Cyprus, you must comply with Cyprus immigration requirements and, where necessary, apply for the appropriate residence permit.

When to Apply for a Cyprus Visa

  • No earlier than 6 months before the travel date
  • No later than 15 days before entry
  • For seafarers, applications may be submitted up to 9 months before the planned trip.
  • Standard processing time — approximately 15 calendar days
  • In complex cases — up to 45 days

Practical Advice from ResidentPravo Specialists

Submit your visa documents 4–6 weeks before your planned trip. This helps reduce the risk of processing delays and provides sufficient time to supply any additional documents that may be requested by the consulate.

Documents Required for a Cyprus Visa

The standard document package for a Short-Stay Visa (Category C) includes:

  • Valid passport
  • Completed and signed visa application form
  • Passport-size photograph meeting the required specifications
  • Proof of accommodation in Cyprus (hotel booking, rental agreement or invitation from the host)
  • Proof of travel arrangements (flight tickets or booking confirmation)
  • Medical insurance valid in the Republic of Cyprus
  • Documents demonstrating sufficient financial means for your stay in Cyprus
  • Bank statements or other documents confirming the applicant's financial situation
  • Documents confirming employment, business activity or another source of income

Passport Requirements

  • The passport must be recognised by the Republic of Cyprus.
  • The passport must contain at least two blank pages for visa stamps.
  • The passport must have been issued within the last 10 years.
  • The passport must remain valid for at least 3 months after the intended date of departure from the Republic of Cyprus.

Can I Enter Cyprus with a Schengen Visa?

Yes. The Republic of Cyprus allows entry without a national Cypriot visa for holders of valid double-entry or multiple-entry Schengen visas, as well as certain residence permits issued by Schengen member states, subject to the applicable immigration requirements.

Important: A national visa issued by the Republic of Cyprus is not a Schengen visa and does not grant the right to enter, transit through, or stay in the Schengen Area.

Entry into Cyprus on a Schengen Visa: Key Points

If you hold a valid double-entry or multiple-entry Category C Schengen visa, you may enter the Republic of Cyprus without obtaining a national Cyprus visa. There is no requirement to have previously entered the Schengen Area — your Schengen visa simply needs to be valid and meet the applicable entry requirements.

Legal entry into the Republic of Cyprus is made through the international airports of Larnaca (LCA) and Paphos (PFO), as well as through the officially recognised seaports of the Republic of Cyprus.

Important: Entry through Ercan Airport, located in Northern Cyprus, is not recognised as lawful entry into the Republic of Cyprus. Using this route may lead to complications with subsequent visa applications, residence permit procedures or other immigration processes.

Does a Visa Guarantee Entry to Cyprus?

No. A visa does not constitute an absolute guarantee of entry. At border control, an officer may check:

  • Purpose of the trip and whether it matches the purpose of the visa.
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel, invitation)
  • Return flight ticket
  • Sufficient financial means
  • Additional documents confirming the purpose and conditions of the trip

We recommend carrying proof of accommodation, a return ticket, and documents confirming that you have sufficient financial means.

Can I Extend My Visa while in Cyprus?

Visa extension is only possible in exceptional circumstances. In cases of force majeure or humanitarian reasons, an extension may be granted without charge. In cases of serious personal circumstances, a fee of €30 may apply.

Family Members of EU Citizens

Third-country nationals who are family members of EU citizens may apply for a visa under a simplified procedure, provided they meet the applicable EU legal requirements.

Do Russian Citizens Need a Visa for Cyprus in 2026?

Yes. Citizens of the Russian Federation require a visa to enter Cyprus unless they hold a valid multiple-entry or double-entry Schengen visa or another document recognised by the Republic of Cyprus for visa-free entry.

The EU–Russia Visa Facilitation Agreement remains suspended. Standard visa application procedures and standard visa fees apply to Russian citizens.

Important Information for Citizens of the Russian Federation

National Cyprus visa applications must be submitted in person at a BLS International visa application centre. Below are the key requirements and practical points to keep in mind.

  • BLS International centres — Moscow, St. Petersburg, Yekaterinburg, Kazan, Novosibirsk, Rostov-on-Don, Samara and Nizhny Novgorod.
  • Biometrics — mandatory from 6 October 2025 for all applicants aged 12 and over (fingerprints and digital photograph). Applications without biometrics will not be accepted.
  • Processing time — typically 10–15 working days; longer in complex cases. Apply well in advance, especially during peak travel season.
  • Fees — consular fee plus BLS service charge. The total for an adult applicant is generally around €120. Confirm current rates before applying.
  • Travel route — there are no direct flights between Russia and Cyprus. Most travellers connect via Istanbul, Dubai or Yerevan. Entry through Ercan Airport (Northern Cyprus) is not recognised as lawful entry and may affect future visa or residence permit applications.
  • Schengen visa — if you plan to enter Cyprus via a non-Schengen country (Turkey, UAE, Armenia, etc.), a single-entry Schengen visa will generally not suffice. A double-entry or multiple-entry visa is typically required.
  • Financial means — proof of funds of approximately €85 per day of stay is advisable. The consulate may also request documents confirming your travel purpose and financial standing.
  • Accommodation — the consulate may verify your booking. Only use genuine, confirmed reservations; unverified bookings can lead to additional requests or refusal.
Additional documents (where applicable)

SFR employment statement — in some cases the consulate may request a statement from Russia's Social Fund (SFR) covering 10 years of employment history. The document must be generated electronically and carry a valid digital signature.

Travel insurance — before purchasing a policy, check that your insurer is on the list accepted by the visa application centre. The accepted provider list is subject to change.

Before submitting your documents, check the current requirements on the official website of your BLS visa centre or the Embassy of the Republic of Cyprus — document lists and procedures may be updated.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Apply for a Cyprus National Visa

Below is a general outline of the process for obtaining a short-stay national visa for the Republic of Cyprus for Russian citizens.

1
Choose Your Application Centre

Applications for a national Cyprus visa must be submitted in person at an authorised BLS International visa application centre. Centres operate across Russia, including in Moscow, Saint Petersburg, Yekaterinburg, Kazan, Novosibirsk, Rostov-on-Don, Krasnodar, Samara, Ufa and Nizhny Novgorod. Personal attendance is mandatory for all applicants.

2
Prepare Your Documents
  • Passport: original and copy; valid for at least 3 months after your intended departure from the Republic of Cyprus; issued no more than 10 years ago.
  • Visa application form: completed in English and signed in person by the applicant.
  • Photograph: colour photo, 3.5 × 4.5 cm, on a plain light background, meeting the current requirements of the Consulate of the Republic of Cyprus.
  • Flight tickets: flight reservation confirmation or itinerary for outbound and return flights.
  • Proof of accommodation: hotel reservation, rental agreement or an official letter of invitation from the host (Assumption of Responsibility), where applicable.
  • Financial documents: evidence of sufficient funds (approx. €85 per day of stay) — for example, bank statements and, where applicable, documents confirming the source of income.
  • Travel medical insurance: policy with a minimum coverage of €30,000, valid for the entire duration of stay in Cyprus.
  • Additional documents: in certain cases the consulate may request further materials, such as a statement from the Russian Social Fund (SFR) confirming employment history for the past 10 years.
3
Attend the Visa Centre
  • Documents must be submitted in person — remote submission is not available
  • All applicants aged 12 and over must provide biometric data (fingerprints and a photograph). This requirement has been in force since October 2025
  • Payment of the consular fee (approx. €90) and the visa centre service fee is required at the time of submission
4
Await the Decision
  • Standard processing time is approximately 10–15 working days
  • In some cases, processing may take longer if the consulate requests additional documents
  • It is advisable to apply at least 4–6 weeks before your planned travel date
5
Collect Your Visa and Verify the Details

Once your visa is issued, check all personal details immediately: name, date of birth, passport series and number, visa validity dates, and the number of permitted entries. Spotting any discrepancy before your trip is much easier than resolving it at border control.

Important

It is advisable to wait until your visa has been issued before purchasing non-refundable flights. The Consulate of the Republic of Cyprus may request additional documents or extend the processing period depending on individual circumstances.

Pitfalls and Mistakes to Avoid

What to Pay Attention To
  • Entry through Ercan Airport (Northern Cyprus) is not recognised as lawful entry into the Republic of Cyprus and may cause problems with future visa or residence permit applications.
  • Do not use fictitious hotel reservations or flight bookings. The consulate may verify the documents you submit.
  • Do not purchase non-refundable flights before your visa has been issued.
  • Check the expiry date of your passport — it must meet the consulate's passport validity requirements.
  • Provide only accurate information in your application form. Errors or false statements may result in a refusal.

Do Ukrainian Citizens Need a Visa for Cyprus in 2026?

Ukrainian citizens holding a valid biometric passport may enter the Republic of Cyprus without a visa for up to 90 days within any 180-day period.

Important: Visa-free entry is permitted only for short-term tourism, business, and private visits. It does not grant the right to work, conduct business activities, or stay in Cyprus on a long-term basis. For long-term residence, an appropriate residence permit is required.

Do Armenian Citizens Need a Visa for Cyprus in 2026?

Yes, Armenian citizens require a visa to enter the Republic of Cyprus. The current visa application fee is €35.

Important: The reduced visa application fee does not waive the standard document requirements or the visa application procedure. The final decision on visa issuance is made by the consular authorities of the Republic of Cyprus.
