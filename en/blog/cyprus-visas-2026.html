Cyprus Visa 2026: Complete Guide to Entry into the Republic of Cyprus

Cyprus is one of the most sought-after destinations for tourists, entrepreneurs, investors, tech professionals, and families considering a move to Europe. This article covers everything you need to know about Cyprus visas in 2026, based on official data from the Republic of Cyprus government.

1. Do You Need a Visa to Enter Cyprus?

Whether you need a visa depends primarily on your nationality. Citizens of EU and EEA (European Economic Area) countries can enter Cyprus freely — no visa required — using a passport or national identity card.

Nationals of third countries may need a visa. The specific list of countries subject to the visa requirement is governed by EU legislation and Cyprus national regulations.

Key fact: Cyprus is a member of the European Union but has not yet fully joined the Schengen Area. A Cyprus visa is only valid within the Republic of Cyprus and does not grant free travel throughout other Schengen countries.

2. What Types of Visas Does Cyprus Issue?

The Republic of Cyprus issues several categories of visas depending on the purpose and length of stay:

  • Airport Transit Visa (Category A) — for transit through the international zone of a Cyprus airport
  • Short-Stay Visa (Category C) — for stays of up to 90 days
  • Multiple Entry Visa (Category C MEV) — a multiple-entry short-stay visa
  • Border Visa — issued at the border in exceptional circumstances
  • Visa Extension — an extension of an existing visa
  • Long-Stay Visa (Category D) — for stays of up to one year

3. Airport Transit Visa (Category A)

An airport transit visa is required for transit through the international zone of a Cyprus airport — without crossing the border or entering the country.

This visa is only required by nationals of specific countries. A number of categories are exempt, including:

  • Holders of valid Schengen visas
  • Holders of residence permits from Schengen Area countries
  • Nationals with valid visas or residence permits from the US, Canada, UK, Japan and certain other countries

4. Short-Stay Visa (Category C)

The Category C visa is the most common type of Cyprus visa. It covers:

  • Tourism
  • Visiting friends and family
  • Business meetings and negotiations
  • Conferences and events
  • Short business trips

Length of stay: up to 90 days within any 180-day period. The 90/180 rule is strictly applied: once you have used 90 days, you cannot re-enter until a new 180-day period begins.

5. Multiple-Entry Visa (Category C MEV)

A multiple-entry Category C visa is issued to those who travel to Cyprus regularly — for business, family visits or other ongoing reasons.

  • Valid for 6 months to 5 years
  • Allows unlimited entries
  • The 90/180-day rule still applies: no more than 90 days in any 180-day period

6. Border Visa

A border visa is issued only in exceptional circumstances — typically for humanitarian or emergency reasons when it was not possible to obtain a visa in advance.

  • Issued at the port of entry
  • Only on serious humanitarian grounds
  • Usually valid for up to 15 days

Warning: Do not rely on obtaining a border visa. If you arrive without a visa, you may be refused entry to Cyprus.

7. Extending Your Visa in Cyprus

Extending a Cyprus visa is possible in limited circumstances and is the exception rather than the rule. Grounds for extension include:

  • Force majeure (natural disaster, accident, loss of documents)
  • Humanitarian reasons
  • Serious personal circumstances (illness, death of a close relative)

Extension fee: €30.

8. Long-Stay Visa (Category D)

A Category D long-stay visa is intended for those planning an extended stay in Cyprus — up to one year. It can be linked to:

  • Employment
  • Study or research
  • Family reunification
  • Long-term residence

A Category D visa is often the first step towards obtaining a Cyprus residence permit. After entering on a long-stay visa, you will need to apply to the Immigration Department for a residence card.

10. When to Apply for a Cyprus Visa

  • No earlier than 6 months before your travel date
  • No later than 15 days before entry
  • For seafarers — applications may be submitted up to 9 months in advance
  • Standard processing time: approximately 15 calendar days
  • In complex cases: up to 45 days

Tip: Apply at least 3–4 weeks before your trip to allow time for any requests for additional documents.

11. Documents Required for a Cyprus Visa

The standard document package for a short-stay visa (Category C) includes:

  • Valid passport
  • Completed and signed visa application form
  • Passport-format photograph
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, invitation letter from host)
  • Return or onward flight tickets (or itinerary receipt)
  • Travel insurance with a minimum coverage of €30,000, including emergency care, hospitalisation and repatriation
  • Proof of sufficient funds (at least €300 for the trip, or equivalent documentation)
  • Bank statements for the last 3 months
  • Documents confirming employment, business or other source of income
  • Invitation or sponsorship form from host (if applicable)
  • Additional documents when travelling with children (birth certificates, notarised consent)

12. Passport Requirements

  • The passport must be recognised by the Republic of Cyprus
  • At least 2 blank pages for visas and stamps
  • Issued no more than 10 years ago
  • Valid for at least 3 months beyond the intended departure date from Cyprus

13. Can You Enter Cyprus with a Schengen Visa?

Yes. Cyprus recognises valid double-entry and multiple-entry Schengen visas, as well as residence permits from certain Schengen Area countries, as grounds for entry without a separate Cyprus visa.

The reverse does not apply: A Cyprus visa is not a Schengen visa and does not entitle the holder to enter or stay in other Schengen countries.

14. Do Russian Citizens Need a Visa for Cyprus in 2026?

Yes. Russian nationals require a visa to enter Cyprus unless they have another valid basis for entry — such as a valid Schengen visa or a residence permit from an EU country.

The EU-Russia simplified visa facilitation agreement has been suspended. This means standard procedures and fees apply to all Russian passport holders.

15. Do Ukrainian Citizens Need a Visa for Cyprus in 2026?

Ukrainian nationals holding a biometric passport enjoy visa-free access to the EU — including Cyprus — for short stays of up to 90 days in any 180-day period.

Important: Visa-free entry covers tourism, business travel and family visits only. It does not permit employment, running a business or long-term residence. A residence permit is required for a longer stay.

16. Do Armenian Citizens Need a Visa for Cyprus in 2026?

Yes. Armenian nationals require a visa to enter Cyprus. However, a reduced visa fee of €35 applies (instead of the standard €90).

You will need to submit the standard set of documents in line with Cyprus requirements.

17. Does a Visa Guarantee Entry into Cyprus?

No. Holding a valid visa does not guarantee entry. At passport control, the border officer is entitled to verify:

  • The purpose of your visit and whether it matches the visa type
  • Confirmation of accommodation
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Sufficient funds for the duration of stay
  • Any other documents at the officer's discretion

Recommendation: Always carry printed copies of your accommodation bookings, tickets and proof of financial means.

18. Visa Refusal

If your visa application is refused, you are entitled to:

  • A written notification of the refusal, including the stated grounds
  • Submit an appeal

Appeal deadline: 75 days from the date of receiving the refusal decision. Appeals are reviewed by the competent authorities of the Republic of Cyprus.

19. Family Members of EU Citizens

Third-country nationals who are family members of an EU citizen benefit from a simplified entry procedure:

  • Reduced documentation requirements
  • Expedited processing
  • No visa fee

Supporting documents will be required to confirm family member status (marriage certificate, birth certificate, etc.).
