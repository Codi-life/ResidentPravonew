Types of Permits

Types of Residence Permits in Cyprus

Cyprus offers several grounds for residency. The right option depends on your situation, budget and goals.

Pink Slip (Temporary Permit)

For residing in Cyprus without the right to work. Issued for up to 1 year with annual renewal options.

  • Timeline: from 2 weeks
  • Requirements: proof of income and address in Cyprus

Residence via Company

For business owners, directors and employees of a Cyprus company, including BCS companies authorised to hire specialists from non-EU countries.

  • Timeline: from 2 months
  • Requirements: company registration or employment by a Cyprus company

Permanent Residency via Investment

Permanent residency in Cyprus through investment in qualifying assets. Issued indefinitely — no annual renewal required.

  • Timeline: 2–4 months
  • Requirements: investment from €300,000

Family Relocation

For spouses, children and dependent family members of a Cyprus resident. Processed alongside the main applicant.

  • Timeline: with the main applicant
  • Requirements: proof of family ties and financial support

Digital Nomad Visa

For remote workers and freelancers with income from employers or clients based outside Cyprus.

  • Timeline: 2–3 months
  • Requirements: income from €3,500/month from abroad

Student Residence Permit

For students enrolled at Cyprus universities, colleges or language schools. Valid for the full duration of study.

  • Timeline: duration of studies
  • Requirements: enrolment at an accredited institution in Cyprus

Work Permit

For qualified professionals with a job offer from a Cyprus employer. IT, finance, healthcare and engineering.

  • Timeline: 1–3 months
  • Requirements: employment contract with a Cyprus company

Property Purchase

Residential or investment property in Cyprus for living, obtaining permanent residency or capital preservation.

  • Timeline: from 1 month
  • Requirements: property selection and legal due diligence

Documents

Documents for Cyprus Residence and Permanent Permits

All programmes require a standard base pack: a valid passport, medical insurance and a criminal record certificate with apostille. Below are the additional documents required for each programme. We assist with apostilles, notarisation, translations and submission.

Residence Permit Through a Cyprus Company

For entrepreneurs, directors and employees of a Cyprus company (Ltd). Documents confirm genuine business activity on the island.

  • Cyprus company incorporation documents
  • Tax Identification Certificate (TIC) and VAT registration
  • Office lease agreement in Cyprus
  • Company bank account statement
  • Employment contract for director
  • Evidence of business activity

Permanent Residency via Investment in Cyprus

For investors committing at least €300,000 to real estate or qualifying assets. Documents confirm the source of funds and the investment itself.

  • Purchase agreement / investment documents
  • Proof of payment from €300,000
  • Proof of Income for the Last 3 Years
  • Bank statement from international bank
  • Source of funds documentation
  • Marriage / birth certificates

Pink Slip — Temporary Residence Permit in Cyprus

For those relocating to Cyprus without the right to work for a local employer. Key requirements are proof of accommodation and financial independence.

  • Rental agreement for accommodation in Cyprus
  • Proof of financial means
  • Bank statement for last 3–6 months
  • Proof of income source

Family Relocation to Cyprus

For spouses, children and dependants of an existing Cyprus resident. Submitted together with the main applicant under any eligible residence programme.

  • Marriage certificate (apostille + translation)
  • Children's birth certificates
  • Criminal record certificates for all family members
  • Medical insurance for each family member
  • Proof of financial support by the main applicant

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Cyprus offers several residency options: the Pink Slip (temporary residence permit for financially independent persons), residency through a Cyprus company registration, permanent residency through investment from €300,000, family reunification, employment-based permits, student visas, digital nomad permits and startup visas. Each programme has its own requirements, timelines and document checklist. The most suitable option depends on your nationality, family status, source of income and business goals.
A residence permit is a temporary permission to live in Cyprus that must be renewed regularly. A company-based permit is renewed annually while the company remains active; the Pink Slip is renewed each year. Permanent residency is an indefinite permit requiring no annual renewal. Permanent residency through investment from €300,000 is the most popular route to long-term residency in Cyprus. If the legal requirements are met, applicants may become eligible for Cypriot citizenship in accordance with the applicable legislation.
To obtain a residence permit through a Cyprus company, you must register a Cyprus Ltd, appoint a director, open a corporate bank account and demonstrate genuine business activity. The application is then submitted to the Cyprus Immigration Department. Processing takes 2–4 months. The permit covers the director, owner and employees of the company, as well as their family members. Company registration in Cyprus takes 5–10 working days.
The standard document pack for all Cyprus residency programmes includes: a valid passport (at least 2 years remaining), biometric photos, medical insurance and a clean criminal record certificate with apostille. For a company-based permit, you also need the company's incorporation documents, tax number (TIC), bank statement and director's employment contract. For permanent residency through investment, a sale and purchase agreement and proof of payment from €300,000 are required. We assist with apostilles, translations and notarisation.
Processing times depend on the programme. Pink Slip: 2–4 weeks. Company-based residence permit: company registration takes 5–10 working days, followed by 2–4 months for the immigration department to process the application. Permanent residency through investment: 2–4 months after a complete package is submitted. Document preparation typically takes 2–4 weeks. We monitor all stages and keep you informed of your application status throughout the process.
Costs vary by permit type. Pink Slip: government fees and document preparation from €500–1,000. Company-based residency: company registration fee plus a service charge. Permanent residency through investment: a minimum investment of €300,000 plus government fees and legal support. The total cost also depends on your nationality, the number of family members and the complexity of your case. Contact us for a personalised cost estimate based on your specific situation.
Yes. Most Cyprus residency programmes extend to the main applicant's family. Under the company-based permit, the director or owner receives the permit along with their spouse and children. Permanent residency through investment also covers the spouse and dependent children. In some cases, parents may be included in the application. All family members must submit the standard document pack: passports, marriage and birth certificates with apostilles.
Requirements vary by permit type. The Pink Slip and company-based permit do not require full-time residence, but proof of connection to the island — such as an active rental agreement or owned property — is needed for renewal. Permanent residency through investment does not require full-time residence, which is one of its key advantages. To obtain Cyprus tax residency status, you must spend at least 183 or 60 days on the island per calendar year, depending on the applicable rule.
Yes, a significant part of the Cyprus company registration process can be completed remotely. Incorporation documents, notarisation and apostilles can be arranged via a power of attorney without visiting Cyprus in person. Most banks require a personal visit to open a corporate account. The residence permit application also requires in-person attendance for biometrics. We manage the entire process and clearly explain what can be done remotely and what requires a trip to Cyprus.
Validity depends on the permit type. The Pink Slip is issued for one year and renewed annually. A company-based residence permit is also renewed annually, provided the company remains active. Permanent residency through investment is indefinite and requires no regular renewal — this is its key advantage over other permit types. If the basis for the permit ceases to exist (e.g. the company is dissolved), the permit is cancelled. We track renewal deadlines and notify clients in advance.
Pink Slip is a temporary residence permit. Category F is a separate permanent residence category. The Pink Slip is issued to persons with confirmed income from abroad and does not permit employment by a Cyprus-based employer. However, a Pink Slip holder may be a director or shareholder of a Cyprus company, receive dividends from the business and carry out entrepreneurial activities through a registered Cyprus company. If you plan to actively work or run a business on the island, a company-based residence permit is likely the more appropriate option for your situation.
Most documents for a Cyprus residence permit can be prepared abroad: apostilles, notarised translations and official certificates can be obtained in your home country, and some applications can be submitted via an authorised representative. However, personal attendance is required for biometrics when collecting the residency card, for opening a bank account and in some cases for submitting documents to the immigration department. We clearly explain what requires a personal visit and help you plan your trip to Cyprus efficiently.
